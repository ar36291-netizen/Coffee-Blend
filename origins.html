Every coffee we carry is sourced from a specific region chosen for what it naturally does — not what processing can make it do. Here's what you're choosing from.

13.3°N, 75.8°E

South Indian

Chikmagalur & Coorg, Karnataka

Grown in the Western Ghats at 900–1700m, Karnataka's estates produce a full-bodied, low-acid coffee with malt and earth undertones. The altitude slows the cherry's development, concentrating sugars without tipping into brightness.

Full Body Low Acid Earthy Malt Chicory-compatible
Altitude
900–1700m

Process
Washed and natural, estate-dependent. Most Chikmagalur lots are washed, preserving the clean, earthy profile over fruit-forward notes.

Ethiopian

Yirgacheffe, SNNPR

Yirgacheffe is widely regarded as one of the world's finest coffee origins. Natural processing lets the cherry dry around the bean, imparting intense berry and fruit notes. Floral aromatics and a lingering, tea-like finish.

Floral Blueberry Bergamot Light Body
Altitude
1700–2200m

Process
Natural (sun-dried). The long drying period on raised beds allows fermentation flavours to develop, giving Yirgacheffe its distinctively fruit-forward character.

1.8°N, 76.1°W

Colombian

Huila, Southern Colombia

Huila sits in the Andes where two mountain ranges converge, creating exceptional microclimates. The result is a balanced, clean cup with caramel sweetness and bright but mild acidity — the definition of an everyday specialty coffee.

Caramel Balanced Mild Acid Clean
Altitude
1500–2000m

Process
Washed. Colombia's wet-milling infrastructure is among the most consistent in the world, which is why Huila lots reliably deliver clarity and sweetness without lot-to-lot variation.

Italian Style

Napoli blend, sourced globally

This isn't a single origin — it's a tradition. The Neapolitan style demands a dark, intense roast of robusta-heavy blends, producing espresso with a thick crema, dark chocolate bitterness, and a creamy body that holds up through milk.

Dark Roast Chocolate Robust Espresso-forward
Blend composition
Robusta-forward with Arabica for balance. Robusta contributes caffeine, crema, and bitterness; Arabica smooths the edges and adds aromatic complexity.
